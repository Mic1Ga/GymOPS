# GymOps · Top 10 Objection Handlers

For each objection · the underlying concern (real or fake) · the response · what NOT to say.

The goal isn't to win the argument. It's to reveal whether the objection is real (close) or a stall (move on).

---

## 1 · "I already use {{ MindBody / Glofox / GymMaster }}"

**Underlying**: comfort with the incumbent · switching cost fear.

**Response**:
> *"Good — keep it. GymOps doesn't replace booking and billing, those are sticky. What it does replace is the marketing + content + retention layer, which most of those tools do badly. You'd run both — MindBody for member management, GymOps for the AI ops."*

**Don't say**: *"You should switch from MindBody."* That triggers their loss-aversion. We coexist.

---

## 2 · "I don't have time to learn another tool"

**Underlying**: real concern — they're already overwhelmed.

**Response**:
> *"Fair. The onboarding is exactly 30 minutes for activation, plus three 15-minute check-ins across two weeks. If you've got 90 minutes total in the next 14 days, you can be operational. If you don't have 90 minutes in 14 days, you genuinely shouldn't buy this — you'd need to delegate it to someone."*

**Don't say**: *"It's super easy!"* No tool is. They've heard it 100 times.

---

## 3 · "It's too expensive"

**Underlying**: either real budget concern OR they don't yet see the value.

**Response (Socratic — make them name the value first)**:
> *"What were you expecting? Out of curiosity — what's the most you've spent on a marketing tool that paid for itself?"*

(Listen. Then anchor against their answer.)

If their answer is "I've never had one pay for itself" → that's the real problem, address that.
If their answer is "I pay X for {{ tool }}" → *"OK, so for $Y/month you get the AI team, retention, content, and no per-seat surprises. Break-even is one captured lead a month at your average member value."*

**Don't say**: *"It pays for itself!"* (Too vague.) Or discount on the spot (signals overpriced).

---

## 4 · "I want to think about it"

**Underlying**: not a yes, not a no, often a polite stall.

**Response**:
> *"Sure. Two questions before you do — what would make it a yes? And when should I check back?"*

If they can answer 1 → that's the real objection · address it now.
If they can't answer 1 → it's a polite no. Move on.

**Don't say**: *"Take all the time you need!"* (Empty pleasantry, signals you're desperate.)

---

## 5 · "I need to discuss with my business partner / spouse"

**Underlying**: either a real DMU complexity OR a deflection.

**Response**:
> *"Totally fair. Want to do a 10-minute call with both of you on Friday? I can pull up the same demo and answer both your questions in one shot — saves you having to be the salesperson tonight."*

**Why this works**: if they say yes, you talk to the actual decision-maker. If they say no, the partner doesn't actually have a vote — it was a deflection.

**Don't say**: *"Let me know what they think!"* (You'll never hear back.)

---

## 6 · "Can you build {{ specific feature }} for us first?"

**Underlying**: anchoring · they want concessions.

**Response**:
> *"If we built that, you'd buy?" (literal · word-for-word)*

If they say yes → real demand · scope and price the work as a separate SOW.
If they hesitate → it's a stall. Their wishlist isn't a real blocker. Bring them back to the demo.

**Don't say**: *"Yes we can do that!"* without scoping. You'll work for free.

---

## 7 · "What if my data gets hacked?"

**Underlying**: legitimate concern · easy to address.

**Response**:
> *"Where your member data lives right now: on a third-party SaaS cloud in [San Francisco / Sydney / Tokyo]. That's the attack surface. With GymOps, your member data lives in your browser on your laptop. To 'hack' it, someone would need physical access to your machine. Lock your laptop, you're more secure than 99% of gyms."*

**Add (if they want detail)**:
> *"You can export a backup JSON file weekly — I'll set up the reminder during onboarding. That's your insurance against the laptop dying."*

---

## 8 · "What about GDPR / privacy law?"

**Underlying**: real concern if they have any EU/UK/Australian members.

**Response**:
> *"Sovereignty makes most of this easier, not harder. Article 12 of the EU AI Act needs an audit log — we have it baked in by SHA-256 chain. PDPA / Privacy Act / etc. require data minimisation and clear retention — your members' data lives only on your device, so cross-border transfer concerns drop out. We'll send you the Data Processing Addendum template before you sign."*

(If they want the actual document — point to `contracts/data-processing-addendum.md` in the pack.)

---

## 9 · "What if the AI generates something embarrassing?"

**Underlying**: real concern · brand risk.

**Response**:
> *"Every AI-generated message is shown to you for review before it sends. We don't auto-publish. The audit chain logs every prompt + output, so if something went wrong you'd see exactly what triggered it. In practice the embarrassing-content risk on the public Claude / OpenAI models is very low — they refuse most edge cases by default. But you, not us, decide what gets published."*

---

## 10 · "What happens when you go out of business?"

**Underlying**: legitimate · sovereignty value-prop.

**Response**:
> *"Best part. The source code is on GitHub — open MIT licence. If we vanished tomorrow, you keep the tool running forever. The AI envelope signing is the only thing tied to us, and even that you can replace by pointing the cascade at your own provider directly — you'd just lose the licence badge. Your members and your work don't stop."*

(This is a strength · own it. Don't get defensive.)

---

## Bonus · Stalls you'll hear (and the close)

**"Send me an email and I'll respond"** → *"Sure — what's the one question I should answer in that email?"* (forces them to name the real concern OR reveals it's a brush-off)

**"Maybe in Q4 / next year"** → *"Fair. What changes by then?"* (if they have a real trigger, calendar a follow-up · if not, they're closing the conversation politely)

**"My current provider is fine"** → *"OK — what's the one thing they don't do that you wish they did?"* (their answer is your sales pitch)

---

## Universal rule

**The first objection is rarely the real one.** Ask "what else?" once before responding. Most owners surface the real concern on the second answer.
