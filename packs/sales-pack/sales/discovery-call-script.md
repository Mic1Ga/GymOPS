# GymOps · 30-Minute Discovery Call Script

**Purpose**: surface the gym's actual pain, qualify them, and book the demo. Or disqualify them — both saves time.
**Length**: 30 minutes max. If you go over, you're talking too much.
**Outcome**: either book demo (next 48 hours) or politely close the conversation.

---

## Pre-call · 5-min prep

- [ ] Find their gym on Google Maps → estimated size, programmes, hours
- [ ] Check their Facebook + Instagram → last post date, post frequency
- [ ] Note 1 thing about them that isn't generic ("Saw you opened a second location in Q3" / "Your Saturday HIIT class looks busy")
- [ ] Have your calendar open for next 48 hours

---

## Minute 0 — 2 · Open

> *"{{ Their name }}, thanks for taking the call. I want to be useful, not waste your time, so here's how this works: I'll ask you 6 questions about how you run the gym, and based on your answers I'll either say 'yeah, you should look at GymOps' or 'honestly, this isn't a fit right now.' Either is fine. Sound OK?"*

(Wait for yes.)

> *"Before I ask — that thing you posted about {{ specific observation }}. {{ Quick comment that shows you actually looked. }}"*

---

## Minute 2 — 18 · The 6 questions

Take notes. Don't sell yet.

### Q1 · Programme + scale
*"Tell me about the gym in 30 seconds — what programmes, how many members, how many staff?"*

(Listen for: number of programmes, whether they have multiple locations, staff size. Also listen for energy — owner-operator who's tired = ideal buyer.)

### Q2 · The pain
*"What's the single most annoying part of running it right now? The thing that, if it disappeared tomorrow, you'd buy yourself a beer about?"*

**THE most important question on the call. Shut up and listen.**

Top answers you'll hear:
- "Replying to enquiries fast enough" → Front Desk specialist
- "Keeping members from quitting" → Retention specialist
- "Posting on Insta consistently" → Content Manager specialist
- "Following up on cold leads" → Sales Assistant specialist
- "Just admin · invoices, reminders, no-shows" → Admin Assistant specialist
- "Nothing — we're growing fine" → Disqualify or revisit later

### Q3 · Current stack
*"What are you using right now to handle that? Software, spreadsheets, your own brain at 11pm?"*

(Listen for: their current tools. Are they paying MindBody/Glofox/GymMaster? Spreadsheets only? Pen and paper? Each is a different pitch.)

### Q4 · The dollars
*"Roughly how much are you spending per month right now on marketing tools + CRM + scheduling combined? Ballpark."*

(They'll either give a number or say "I dunno, a few hundred." Either way, you now have a price anchor.)

### Q5 · The trigger
*"What made you take this call? What's changed in the last 30 days?"*

(Listen for: hired/fired someone, opened a location, lost X members, the new year cohort dropping off, etc. This is the BUY trigger — they don't take calls without one.)

### Q6 · The decision
*"If you decided GymOps was worth trying — would that be a decision you'd make on your own, or would you want to run it past someone?"*

(If they say "I'll just decide" → solo DM, easy. If they say spouse/business partner/etc → get them on the demo too.)

---

## Minute 18 — 25 · Position + decide

Based on what they said, pick the right pitch:

**If they're paying $200-500+/month for MindBody/Glofox + a CRM + Canva Pro + ChatGPT separately:**
> *"OK so you're paying around $X total. GymOps replaces the marketing, CRM, and content pieces — it doesn't replace the booking/billing of MindBody, you keep those. People in your situation save $100-300/month and reclaim about 8-12 hours/week on content + retention. Want to see what that looks like for {{ their gym }}?"*

**If they're on spreadsheets + free tools:**
> *"OK so you're basically doing this in your head and in Excel. Most owners I talk to don't realise how much that's costing in lost enquiries — usually 25-30% of leads that come in after 9pm or on Sundays get cold. GymOps gives you 24/7 capture without paying a Western SaaS price. Want to see a 15-minute demo?"*

**If they're running CrossFit / boutique / specialised:**
> *"You're not the audience MindBody was built for — those are global chains. You need something that learns your tone of voice. GymOps does that, and you can show me a Wednesday 6am class post and I'll have the AI write next week's in 30 seconds. 15-minute demo?"*

**If they're in Asia/non-English market:**
> *"Most of the big platforms charge in USD or AUD whether you make it or not. GymOps you buy once or pay monthly without a 12-month lock-in, and the AI does Thai/Bahasa/Tagalog as natively as English. 15-minute demo so you can see your language?"*

---

## Minute 25 — 30 · Book or close

**If they want the demo:**
> *"Great. Calendar's open right now. Thursday 10am your time or Friday 2pm?"*

(Force a choice between two specific slots. Do not say "when's good for you?")

- [ ] Book demo
- [ ] Send calendar invite within 10 minutes of the call
- [ ] Reply with: "Pulled up your gym already — I'll have the demo personalised to {{ their main programme }}. See you Thursday."

**If they say "send me some info":**
> *"Sure. I'll send a one-pager. Quick question — when you read it, what would make you reply 'let's do the demo' vs 'not for me'?"*

(Their answer tells you the actual objection. Address it in the email you send.)

**If they're not a fit:**
> *"Honestly, based on what you said, I don't think GymOps is your priority right now. {{ Reason: too small / too big / wrong programme / not painful enough. }} If that changes in 3-6 months, drop me a line. No follow-up calls from me — your time matters."*

(Treat them well. They refer the next call.)

---

## Post-call · 10 min

- [ ] Log call in your CRM (or a simple Google Sheet)
- [ ] Note: pain mentioned, current stack, current spend, trigger, decision-maker
- [ ] Calendar invite + agenda email if demo booked
- [ ] One-pager + personal note if "send me info"
- [ ] Clean "not a fit" close note in your CRM with the 3-6 month follow-up date

---

## Red flags · don't book the demo

- They say "we just need a website" → wrong product
- They want enterprise-style RFP with 6 signatures → wrong segment
- They want a free pilot for 6 months → not committed
- They keep asking about features that aren't in GymOps (POS, accounting, payroll) → wrong product
- They say "can you make it look like {{ specific competitor }}?" → they want what's already on the market
