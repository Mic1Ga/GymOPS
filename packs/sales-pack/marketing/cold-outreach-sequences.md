# GymOps · Cold Outreach Sequences

Three sequences. Use the one that matches the channel.

**Rule**: never send more than 3 messages before they reply. If they ghost 3 in a row, they're a no. Send the no-fit close, save 2 hours.

---

## Sequence A · LinkedIn DM (gym owners in APAC)

### DM 1 (Day 0)
> Hey {{ first_name }} — saw your post about {{ specific thing }}. Quick question: how do you currently handle the leads that come in at 9pm after the gym's closed? Asking because most owners I talk to say that's where they bleed the most enquiries. No pitch — just curious if it's a problem for you too.

### DM 2 (Day 3 · only if no reply)
> Wanted to double-check this one landed. The reason I asked is I've been building a tool that replies to enquiries 24/7 — runs in your browser, no monthly SaaS fees, your data never leaves your laptop. Works in Thai, Bahasa, Tagalog as well as English. If after-hours leads are a problem I'd love to show you in 15 mins. If not, all good — won't bug you again.

### DM 3 (Day 7 · final · sets up the close)
> Last note from me. I usually stop reaching out after 3 messages — your inbox is yours. If gym ops + lead capture isn't on your priority list right now, no offence taken. If it is and the timing isn't right, drop me a line in 3 months and I'll still be here. Either way, good luck with {{ their gym }}.

- *"After-hours leads at {{ gym name }}"*
- *"Costs of MindBody/Glofox vs running it sovereign"*
- *"15-min demo — your team, your tone, your laptop"*
- *"3 minutes on the GymOps idea"*

## Sequence C · Facebook Group / WhatsApp group reply

When you see a gym owner post a question in a Facebook group ("anyone using X for Y?"):

> Hey {{ name }} — I make a sovereign tool that handles this. Different angle from {{ tool they asked about }}: runs in your browser, no monthly fees, AI learns your gym's tone. Happy to show in 15 mins if useful. Drop me a DM.

**Rules for groups**:
- Only post if the question is genuinely about what GymOps solves
- Never copy-paste the same response — write it fresh each time
- Always reply to the person asking, not as a top-level post (group admins hate that)
- Don't include links unless asked — credibility > clicks

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Mass-email a scraped list. Bad for deliverability, bad for brand.
- ❌ Send the same template to 20 owners — they're on LinkedIn together, they'll compare notes.
- ❌ Pitch features in the first message. Pitch a question or an observation.
- ❌ Send "just checking in" emails. They're filler. Either you have a reason to write or you don't.
- ❌ Discount before they've asked the price. You'll signal it's overpriced.
